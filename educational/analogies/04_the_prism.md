# Analogy 04 — The Prism: How Localization Produces Three Spatial Components

## The Analogy

White light passing through a prism produces three primary color components — red,
green, blue. The three components were always all present in the white light; the
prism reveals a latent structure that was there before it was made distinguishable.
Anything that interacts with the light after the prism can be characterized by its
RGB values — how much it participates in each component.

D3 localization behaves similarly. The substrate, as it reaches D3 compression depth,
produces three symmetric, equivalent spatial components from what was previously
undifferentiated substrate behavior. Anything interacting with the substrate at or
beyond D3 depth can be characterized by how much it participates in each of the three
components — which is what spatial coordinates are.

---

## What This Analogy Captures

- One undifferentiated thing becomes three symmetric, named components at a threshold.
- The three components were latent before the threshold — not created by it.
- All three components are equivalent (no RGB color is "more real" than another;
  no spatial direction is privileged).
- Anything at or beyond the threshold is automatically characterized by all three
  components simultaneously.
