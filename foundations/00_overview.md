# Overview: The Dimensional Folding Model

## What This Model Claims

The Standard Model of particle physics — its three forces, its particle content, its mass spectrum,
the number of particle generations — is entirely determined by the shape of twelve compactified
extra dimensions. You do not need to put these features in by hand. They follow from the geometry.

Specifically:

- **Electromagnetism** comes from a circle (U(1) fiber)
- **The weak force** comes from a 3-sphere (SU(2) fiber, isomorphic to S³)
- **The strong force** comes from the group manifold of SU(3) (an 8-dimensional fiber)
- These three fibers sit at every point in ordinary 4D spacetime, multiplied together
- The twelve extra dimensions total: 1 + 3 + 8 = 12
- The full space is: 4D spacetime × U(1) × S³ × SU(3)

## What This Model Does Not Claim

- It does not replace General Relativity. GR governs the 4D base spacetime as usual.
- It does not replace quantum mechanics. The quantum structure of the Standard Model remains.
- It does not predict gravity — gravity is the geometry of the base spacetime, not the fibers.
- It is not yet complete. Several quantitative predictions require further development.

## The Central Insight

The three Standard Model forces are not three fragments of a single broken symmetry (as in SU(5)
grand unification). They are three independent geometric spaces that happen to coexist at every
point in the universe.

The analogy: think of a garden hose viewed from far away. It looks like a 1D line. Up close, you
see it is a 2D cylinder — the extra circle exists at every point along the line, but is too small
to see from a distance. Now extend that idea: there are twelve extra shapes, not one, each
associated with a different force, each too small to directly detect.

What makes this model distinctive is that the three extra shapes are kept *separate* — they don't
merge into one bigger shape. This is the product geometry choice, and it has deep consequences.

## The Compactification Scale

The extra dimensions are curled at a scale:

```
L_extra ~ 10^-35 m
```

For comparison:
- Proton radius: ~10^-15 m
- Planck length: ~1.6 × 10^-35 m

The extra dimensions are therefore near the Planck scale — roughly a billion billion times smaller
than a proton. This explains why no experiment has directly detected them. The energies required to
probe that scale exceed current accelerator capabilities by many orders of magnitude.
