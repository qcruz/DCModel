## Observation

Unstable nuclei decay via: alpha (emit He-4 nucleus), beta-minus (n → p + e⁻ + ν̄_e),
beta-plus (p → n + e⁺ + ν_e), or gamma (emit high-energy photon). Each has characteristic
half-lives ranging from fractions of a second to billions of years. Beta decay is mediated
by the weak force; alpha by the strong and EM; gamma by EM.

---

## Standard Explanation

Alpha: quantum tunneling through the Coulomb barrier. Beta: weak force via W-boson exchange
(intra-D6 in DFC). Gamma: electromagnetic transition between nuclear energy levels.
