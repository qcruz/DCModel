## Observation

CP (charge-parity) symmetry is violated in weak decays of kaons and B mesons. The
CP-violating phase in the CKM matrix (δ ≈ 1.2 rad) is the Standard Model source. The
observed baryon asymmetry of the universe requires much larger CP violation than the SM
provides — this is one of the unsolved problems of modern physics.

---

## Standard Explanation

CP violation enters the Standard Model through the complex phase of the CKM matrix for
quarks and PMNS for leptons. It requires at least three generations (Kobayashi-Maskawa
1973, Nobel 2008).
