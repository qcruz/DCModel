## Observation

Atomic nuclei are bound by the residual strong force — a short-range (< 2 fm) attractive
force between nucleons mediated by pion exchange. Nuclear binding energies are ~8 MeV per
nucleon. Iron-56 is the most tightly bound nucleus. Fusion releases energy below iron;
fission releases energy above iron.

---

## Standard Explanation

The nucleon-nucleon force is a residual effect of QCD, analogous to how van der Waals
forces are residual electromagnetic effects. Yukawa (1935) proposed it as pion exchange.
The binding energy curve peaks at Fe-56 due to the competition between nuclear attraction
and electromagnetic repulsion between protons.
