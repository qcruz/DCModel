## Observation

The strong force appears to conserve CP symmetry (the combined symmetry of charge
conjugation and parity reversal) to extraordinary precision:

- **Expected CP violation:** The QCD Lagrangian contains a term — the topological charge
  density of the gluon field — that violates CP symmetry when multiplied by the vacuum
  angle theta. The topological charge density is the gluon field strength tensor contracted
  with its Hodge dual, integrated over spacetime. For any nonzero value of theta, this
  term would produce a neutron electric dipole moment proportional to theta.
- **Experimental constraint:** The neutron electric dipole moment has been measured to be
  less than 1.8 × 10⁻²⁶ electron-charge-centimeters (nEDM collaboration, 2020). Combined
  with the theoretical estimate that the neutron electric dipole moment is approximately
  3.6 × 10⁻¹⁶ theta electron-charge-centimeters, this constrains:

  ```
  |d_n| < 1.8 × 10⁻²⁶ e·cm
  |theta_QCD| < 5 × 10⁻¹¹   [from d_n ≈ 3.6 × 10⁻¹⁶ × theta_QCD e·cm]
  ```

- **The fine-tuning:** There is no symmetry in the Standard Model that forces theta-QCD
  to zero or to any particular small value. The observed smallness requires either
  fine-tuning of the initial conditions or a new physics mechanism.
- **Axion solution (Peccei-Quinn, 1977):** A new symmetry (PQ symmetry) promotes theta-QCD
  to a dynamical field whose potential is minimized at zero. The corresponding
  Nambu-Goldstone boson is the axion, which relaxes to the CP-conserving minimum. Axions
  are a dark matter candidate; no direct detection yet.
- **Current axion searches:** ADMX, ABRACADABRA, CASPEr, HAYSTAC, and related experiments
  are actively searching for axions in the predicted mass range. No detection as of 2025.

## Standard Explanation

In the Standard Model, theta-QCD is a free parameter. Vacuum tunneling transitions between
different QCD vacuum sectors — instantons — relate the physical theta-bar to the sum of
theta-QCD and the complex phase of the quark mass matrix. Because quark masses are complex
numbers in general, there is a physical combination theta-bar that cannot be removed by
field redefinitions.

The Peccei-Quinn mechanism introduces a global U(1) symmetry that is spontaneously broken
at a scale f_a. The resulting pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson (the axion) has a potential
generated by non-perturbative QCD effects (instantons) that is minimized at theta-bar = 0.
The axion mass is of order the QCD scale squared divided by f_a.

What the SM does not explain: why there should be a Peccei-Quinn symmetry, why theta-QCD
was not fine-tuned to an arbitrary value by initial conditions, or whether the axion
actually exists. The axion mechanism solves the fine-tuning dynamically but introduces
a new unexplained symmetry (PQ) and a new unexplained particle (the axion itself).

