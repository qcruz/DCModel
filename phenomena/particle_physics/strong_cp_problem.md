# Phenomenon: The Strong CP Problem

## One-Sentence Synthesis

> The strong CP problem — why the strong force conserves CP symmetry even though the
> QCD Lagrangian permits a CP-violating term proportional to theta-QCD that could be
> any value from zero to two pi, yet is observed to be less than 10⁻¹⁰ — may be
> dissolved in DFC if the D7 SU(3) closure locks into a topologically CP-symmetric
> configuration during its formation event, making theta-QCD structurally zero rather
> than a fine-tuned free parameter, though this topological argument is not yet formalized.

---

## Observation

The strong force appears to conserve CP symmetry (the combined symmetry of charge
conjugation and parity reversal) to extraordinary precision:

- **Expected CP violation:** The QCD Lagrangian contains a term proportional to theta-QCD
  times (g-s squared / 32 pi squared) times G-mu-nu times G-tilde-mu-nu (the gluon field
  strength contracted with its dual). This term violates CP symmetry. For a generic
  value of theta-QCD, it would produce a neutron electric dipole moment of order
  10⁻¹⁶ theta-QCD electron-charge-centimeters.
- **Experimental constraint:** The neutron electric dipole moment has been measured to be
  less than 1.8 × 10⁻²⁶ electron-charge-centimeters (nEDM collaboration, 2020). This
  constrains theta-QCD < 10⁻¹⁰.
- **The fine-tuning:** There is no symmetry in the SM that forces theta-QCD to zero or to
  any particular small value. The observed smallness requires either fine-tuning of the
  initial conditions or a new physics mechanism.
- **Axion solution (Peccei-Quinn, 1977):** A new symmetry (PQ symmetry) promotes theta-QCD
  to a dynamical field whose potential is minimized at zero. The corresponding Nambu-Goldstone
  boson is the axion, which relaxes to the CP-conserving minimum. Axions are a dark matter
  candidate; no direct detection yet.

## Standard Explanation

In the SM, theta-QCD is a free parameter. Vacuum tunneling transitions between different
QCD vacua (instantons) relate the physical theta-bar to the sum of theta-QCD and the
complex phase of the quark mass matrix. Because quark masses are complex numbers in general,
there is a physical combination theta-bar that cannot be removed by field redefinitions.

The Peccei-Quinn mechanism introduces a global U(1) symmetry that is spontaneously broken
at a scale f-a. The resulting pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson (the axion) has a potential
generated by non-perturbative QCD effects (instantons) that is minimized at theta-bar = 0.
The axion mass is of order the QCD scale squared divided by f-a.

What the SM does not explain: why there should be a Peccei-Quinn symmetry, why theta-QCD
was not fine-tuned to an arbitrary value by initial conditions, or whether the axion
actually exists.
