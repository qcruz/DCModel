## Observation

The strong force binds quarks into hadrons and holds atomic nuclei together. It is the
strongest of the four forces at short range (< 1 fm). Unlike other forces, it grows with
distance — quarks cannot be separated (confinement). At very short range, the coupling
weakens (asymptotic freedom). The residual strong force between nucleons holds nuclei
together via pion exchange.

---

## Standard Explanation

Quantum chromodynamics (QCD) — an SU(3) gauge theory. Gluons carry color charge and
self-interact, causing the coupling to grow with distance (confinement) and shrink at
high energy (asymptotic freedom). Confinement has not been analytically proven from QCD —
only demonstrated numerically. Strong force is 100× stronger than EM at 1 fm.
