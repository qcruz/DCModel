## Observation

The weak force mediates radioactive beta decay (n → p + e⁻ + ν̄_e) and all flavor-changing
quark transitions. It is carried by massive W⁺, W⁻, Z⁰ bosons (m_W ≈ 80 GeV). It
violates parity (P) — the weak force only couples to left-handed particles (right-handed
particles are unaffected). It also violates CP symmetry in certain processes.

---

## Standard Explanation

SU(2)_L gauge theory, unified with U(1)_Y into the electroweak theory. The left-handed
subscript L means only left-handed fermions participate. Masses arise from Higgs mechanism.
Parity violation is built in — left and right chiralities are treated asymmetrically.
