## Observation

All fundamental particles that interact with the Higgs field acquire mass proportional
to the strength of that coupling. The W and Z gain mass ~80-91 GeV; the top quark 173 GeV;
the electron 0.511 MeV. Massless particles (photon, gluons) do not couple to the Higgs.
The Higgs VEV v = 246 GeV sets the overall mass scale.

---

## Standard Explanation

Yukawa coupling: L_Yukawa = −y_f φ_H ψ̄_f ψ_f. When φ_H acquires its VEV v, this becomes
a mass term m_f = y_f v/√2. The coupling y_f is a free parameter for each fermion —
the Standard Model does not predict the mass hierarchy.
