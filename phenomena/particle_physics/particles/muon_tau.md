## Observation

The muon (μ) and tau (τ) are heavier copies of the electron — identical quantum numbers,
different masses. The muon decays (μ → e + ν_μ + ν̄_e) with lifetime τ_μ = 2.197 μs.
The tau decays rapidly (τ_τ = 2.9 × 10⁻¹³ s). Their masses:
m_μ = 105.66 MeV (206× electron), m_τ = 1776.9 MeV (3477× electron).

---

## Standard Explanation

Second and third generation charged leptons. Masses from Yukawa couplings to Higgs
(y_μ ≈ 6 × 10⁻⁴, y_τ ≈ 0.01). No Standard Model explanation for the mass ratios.
The muon anomalous magnetic moment (g−2) is currently measured 4.2σ above SM prediction —
one of the strongest hints of physics beyond the Standard Model.
