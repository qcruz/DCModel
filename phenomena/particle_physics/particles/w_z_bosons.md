## Observation

The W⁺, W⁻, and Z⁰ bosons are the massive gauge bosons of the weak force.
Masses: m_W = 80.4 GeV, m_Z = 91.2 GeV. The W bosons are charged; the Z is neutral.
They mediate all flavor-changing and left-handed weak interactions. Their masses arise
from the Higgs mechanism (spontaneous symmetry breaking of SU(2)×U(1)).

---

## Standard Explanation

SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y gauge symmetry is spontaneously broken to U(1)_EM by the Higgs VEV.
This gives mass to W⁺, W⁻, Z while leaving the photon massless. The weak mixing angle
θ_W (sin²θ_W ≈ 0.231) sets the W/Z mass ratio.
