## Observation

Protons (uud, mass 938.3 MeV) and neutrons (udd, mass 939.6 MeV) are the stable bound
states of quarks. Pions (quark-antiquark pairs) are the lightest mesons; they mediate the
residual nuclear force between nucleons. Hundreds of hadron species exist; all are
color-neutral (color-singlet) combinations of quarks and gluons.

---

## Standard Explanation

Quantum chromodynamics (QCD) confines quarks and gluons into color-neutral hadrons. The
proton mass is mostly from gluon field energy and quark kinetic energy (not quark rest
masses). The residual strong force between protons and neutrons is mediated by pion exchange.
