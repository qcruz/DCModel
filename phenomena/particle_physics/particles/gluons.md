## Observation

Gluons are the eight massless gauge bosons of the strong force. They carry color charge
themselves (unlike photons which are electrically neutral) — each gluon has a color-anticolor
combination. Because gluons self-interact, the strong force grows with distance (confinement)
and weakens at short distances (asymptotic freedom). Gluons have never been observed in
isolation.

---

## Standard Explanation

SU(3) gauge theory (QCD). The 8 gluons correspond to the 8 generators of SU(3). Their
self-coupling arises from the non-Abelian structure of SU(3). Asymptotic freedom was
the 2004 Nobel Prize. Confinement is established numerically (lattice QCD) but not
analytically proven.
