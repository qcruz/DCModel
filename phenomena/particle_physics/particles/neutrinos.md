## Observation

Neutrinos are nearly massless, electrically neutral fermions. They interact only via the
weak force. Three flavors exist (electron, muon, tau neutrino), corresponding to the three
generations. Neutrinos oscillate between flavors as they travel — a quantum interference
effect that requires non-zero mass. Measured mass differences: Δm²₂₁ ≈ 7.5 × 10⁻⁵ eV²,
Δm²₃₂ ≈ 2.5 × 10⁻³ eV². Sum of masses: Σm_ν < 0.12 eV (cosmological bound).

---

## Standard Explanation

Neutrinos have no right-handed component in the Standard Model (they are purely
left-handed). Their masses, discovered via oscillation experiments, are not accommodated
by the minimal Standard Model and require extensions (see-saw mechanism, Majorana masses).
The PMNS matrix encodes mixing between flavor and mass eigenstates, analogous to CKM for
quarks.
