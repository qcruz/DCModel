## Observation

Every particle has an antiparticle with equal mass but opposite charges. Matter and
antimatter annihilate on contact, converting entirely to energy (photons). The observable
universe is made almost entirely of matter — the baryon asymmetry η_B ≈ 6 × 10⁻¹⁰
(one extra baryon per ~10¹⁰ photons) requires an explanation. CP violation has been
observed in kaon and B-meson systems.

---

## Standard Explanation

CPT theorem: any Lorentz-invariant quantum field theory conserves CPT (charge conjugation
× parity × time reversal). Antimatter is the CPT-conjugate of matter. The baryon asymmetry
requires CP violation + baryon number violation + departure from thermal equilibrium
(Sakharov conditions). The Standard Model has insufficient CP violation to explain the
observed asymmetry.
