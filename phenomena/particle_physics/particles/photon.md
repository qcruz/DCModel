# Phenomenon: The Photon

## One-Sentence Synthesis

> The photon is the massless excitation of the U(1) closure at D5 — the gauge boson of
> electromagnetism — whose properties (zero mass, spin 1, E = hν) all follow from being
> the massless compression wave in the D2 propagation regime with a U(1) fold orientation.

---

## Observation

Photons are massless, travel at c, carry energy E = hν and momentum p = E/c. They have
spin 1 with two helicity states (±1). They are emitted and absorbed by charged particles.
They mediate the electromagnetic force. See `phenomena/light/light.md` for the complete
treatment.

---

## Standard Explanation

The photon is the gauge boson of U(1)_EM. Its masslessness is protected by gauge symmetry.
It couples to all electrically charged particles with strength proportional to charge e.

---

## Dimensional Folding Explanation

The photon is fully treated in `phenomena/light/light.md`. Summary: the photon is the
massless limit of the compression wave (V'(φ) = 0 — no buckling potential), propagating
as a D2 mode. Its U(1) gauge structure comes from the D5 closure topology; its fold
orientation angle θ is the polarization. E = hν follows from the massless KG dispersion.
