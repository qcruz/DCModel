## Observation

Discovered at CERN in 2012. Mass m_H = 125.25 GeV. Spin 0. Couples to all massive
particles with strength proportional to mass. Decays predominantly to bb̄ (58%), WW* (21%),
gg (9%), τ⁺τ⁻ (6%), ZZ* (4%). The Higgs field has a non-zero vacuum expectation value
v = 246 GeV that spontaneously breaks the electroweak symmetry.

---

## Standard Explanation

The Higgs field is a complex SU(2) doublet. Its potential V = −μ²|H|² + λ|H|⁴ has a
minimum at |H| = v/√2. Spontaneous symmetry breaking gives mass to W, Z, and fermions.
The Higgs boson is the remaining physical degree of freedom after SSB. The Standard Model
does not explain why μ² > 0 (the hierarchy problem: why is the Higgs so light?).
