## Observation

The observable universe contains ~10¹⁰ photons for every baryon. This means that at the
Big Bang, baryons and antibaryons were nearly but not exactly equal — one extra baryon
per ~10¹⁰ pairs. Without this asymmetry, all matter and antimatter would have annihilated
and the universe would contain only photons. Sakharov (1967) identified the three
conditions required: baryon number violation, C and CP violation, departure from thermal
equilibrium.

---

## Standard Explanation

No Standard Model mechanism produces sufficient CP violation. Extensions include
electroweak baryogenesis (sphaleron processes), leptogenesis (CP violation in neutrino
sector), and Affleck-Dine baryogenesis. None is confirmed.
