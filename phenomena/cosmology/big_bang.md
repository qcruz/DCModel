# Phenomenon: The Big Bang

## One-Sentence Synthesis

> The Big Bang is not a creation event but the first major buckling instability of a

## Observation

The universe began approximately 13.8 billion years ago in an extremely hot, dense state
and has been expanding and cooling since. Evidence:
- Cosmic microwave background (CMB): near-perfect blackbody spectrum at T = 2.725 K,
  uniform to 1 part in 10⁵ across the sky
- Hubble's law: galaxies recede at rates proportional to distance
- Big Bang nucleosynthesis: primordial H/He/Li abundances match predictions from T ~ 1 MeV
- Large-scale structure: galaxy distribution consistent with seeding by early perturbations
- The flatness of the universe: spatial curvature Ω_k ≈ 0 to within observational precision

---

## Standard Explanation

General Relativity + standard cosmology: the Friedmann equations describe an expanding
spacetime with scale factor a(t). Inflation (exponential expansion at t ≲ 10⁻³² s) solves
the flatness and horizon problems by expanding a causally connected patch to cosmological
scales. After inflation, the universe cooled through a sequence of phase transitions:

- T ~ 10¹⁵ GeV: electroweak symmetry breaking (or earlier, depending on model)
- T ~ 200 MeV: QCD confinement, quarks bind into hadrons
- T ~ 1 MeV: Big Bang nucleosynthesis, light nuclei form
- T ~ 0.3 eV (z ~ 1100): recombination, CMB decouples, universe becomes transparent

The standard model provides no mechanism for why the initial singularity occurred or what
determined the initial conditions.

**The dimensional stack assembled in sequence, not all at once.**

## Resolution of Standard Cosmological Problems

### Flatness Problem

The flatness problem asks: why is Ω_k ≈ 0 to extraordinary precision, requiring the
initial density to equal the critical density to 1 part in 10⁶⁰?

In DFC, the flatness problem dissolves structurally:

The near-D1 compressed state has no spatial structure by definition — it is approaching
zero degrees of spatial differentiation. There is no spatial curvature in D1 because
spatial geometry has not yet emerged. The first bifurcation opens degrees of freedom into
a state that is, by construction, the flattest possible starting point: zero curvature is
not a fine-tuned initial condition, it is the necessary state of a substrate that has not
yet established any spatial geometry.

The apparent flat universe is flat because spatial geometry emerged from a compression
state that had none.

### Horizon Problem

The horizon problem asks: how can regions of the CMB that were causally disconnected at
recombination be at the same temperature to 1 part in 10⁵?

In DFC, the horizon problem dissolves structurally:

The substrate is one continuous object. Before D3 localization produced apparent spatial
separation, there was no spatial distance — the substrate was connected by construction.
Correlations across what appear as large spatial separations in D3 are correlations within
a single object that predate the emergence of spatial separation itself. The uniformity of
the CMB reflects the uniformity of the pre-bifurcation compressed substrate: at maximum
compression, the substrate has no spatial structure and therefore no spatial variation.

Causal disconnection is a property of the D3 localization behavior. It applies only after
D3 has formed. Applying it to the pre-D3 compressed substrate is a category error.

### What This Model Does NOT Resolve (Inflation Not Required, But Not Derived Either)

The DFC structural resolutions of flatness and horizon do not require an inflationary
epoch. However, inflation was also introduced to explain the origin of the density
perturbations that seed large-scale structure (the CMB temperature fluctuations and the
distribution of galaxies). DFC currently has no account of what determines the amplitude
and spectrum of these primordial perturbations. Whether DFC produces a Harrison-Zel'dovich
power spectrum (scale-invariant, as observed) from compression dynamics is an open derivation.

## Open Questions

1. **What determines the scale of the first bifurcation?** What substrate parameter
   (α, β, c) sets the threshold at which the near-D1 state buckles? The first bifurcation
   scale is presumably the Planck scale from the kink width identification, but this needs
   to be derived directly from the compression field equation.

2. **Origin of the primordial density perturbation spectrum.** DFC must produce scale-
   invariant perturbations from compression dynamics to replace the inflationary mechanism.
   Whether the buckling cascade naturally generates a Harrison-Zel'dovich spectrum is not
   known.

3. **Why does the bifurcation sequence proceed in the observed order** D2 → D3 → D4 →
   D5 → D6 → D7? The ordering of closure thresholds is an input to the model; it must be
   derived from the compression field dynamics.

4. **Cyclic or singular?** The near-D1 compressed state is described as the substrate
   approaching D1 asymptotically — the substrate always existed, compressing. This implies
   the "Big Bang" is not the universe's origin but the first observable threshold crossing.
   What was the substrate doing before? Is there a cyclic compression-and-bifurcation
   history? This is not currently addressed.

5. **Connection to dark matter and dark energy.** The D4–D5 intermediate kinks (dark matter
   candidates) and the global compression rate (dark energy analog) must both be consistent
   with the primordial production history.
