## Observation

The universe began ~13.8 billion years ago in an extremely hot, dense state. It has been
expanding and cooling ever since. Evidence: cosmic microwave background (CMB), Big Bang
nucleosynthesis (hydrogen/helium/lithium abundances), Hubble's law (galaxies receding),
and the observed homogeneity and isotropy of the universe on large scales.

---

## Standard Explanation

GR + standard cosmology: the Friedmann equations describe the expanding universe. Inflation
(exponential expansion in the first ~10⁻³² s) solves the flatness and horizon problems.
The universe cooled from a quark-gluon plasma to hadron formation at T ~ 200 MeV,
then nucleosynthesis at T ~ 1 MeV, then recombination at T ~ 0.3 eV (z ~ 1100).
