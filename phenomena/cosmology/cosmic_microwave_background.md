## Observation

The CMB is thermal radiation filling all of space at T = 2.725 K, left over from when the
universe cooled enough for electrons and protons to combine into neutral hydrogen (~380,000
years after the Big Bang, z ≈ 1100). It is a near-perfect blackbody spectrum. Tiny
temperature anisotropies (ΔT/T ~ 10⁻⁵) encode the density fluctuations that seeded all
large-scale structure.

---

## Standard Explanation

Relic photons from the surface of last scattering. The blackbody spectrum is the
Planck distribution n(ν) = 1/(e^{hν/kT} − 1) at T = 2.725 K. Anisotropies arise from
acoustic oscillations in the baryon-photon fluid before recombination.
