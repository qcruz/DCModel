# Phenomenon: Cosmic Expansion and the Accelerating Universe

## One-Sentence Synthesis

> Cosmic expansion is not the universe moving outward into pre-existing space — it is

## Observation

The universe is expanding. Distant galaxies recede at velocities proportional to their
distance (Hubble's law). The expansion is not uniform over time — measurements of Type Ia
supernovae established in 1998 that the expansion is *accelerating*. The Hubble constant
measured from the early universe (CMB, H₀ ≈ 67.4 km/s/Mpc) disagrees with measurements
from the local universe (distance ladder, H₀ ≈ 73 km/s/Mpc) by approximately 5σ. No
Standard Model explanation resolves this tension.

---

## Standard Explanation

General Relativity describes cosmic expansion through the Friedmann equations, derived from
the Einstein field equations applied to a homogeneous, isotropic universe (FLRW metric).
The expansion rate is determined by the energy content:

```
H² = (8πG/3)(ρ_matter + ρ_radiation + ρ_Λ)
```

Acceleration requires a component with negative pressure — dark energy. The simplest
candidate is the cosmological constant Λ (vacuum energy with equation of state w = −1).
Dark energy accounts for ≈68% of the total energy budget.

What standard cosmology does not explain:
- Why Λ has the value it does (120 orders of magnitude below naive quantum field theory
  estimates — the cosmological constant problem)
- Why matter and dark energy are comparable in density today (coincidence problem)
- Why H₀ measurements from early and late epochs disagree (Hubble tension)
- What dark energy physically is

## Dimensional Folding Explanation

### Abstract Level

**The universe is not expanding into anything.**

Expansion, in this model, is a description of what lateral redistribution looks like from
inside the D3 localization layer. When the global compression field removes dimensional
volume (vertical collapse toward D1), the remaining structure cannot simply contract — it
redistributes sideways through the remaining dimensional space. That sideways redistribution,
observed from within D3 by structures anchored at D4+, appears as increasing separation.

The recession of galaxies is not motion through pre-existing space. It is the continuous
re-tiling of the D3 localization layer as dimensional volume is removed beneath it.

**Dark energy is the compression pressure itself.**

The cosmological constant Λ is not a mysterious repulsive substance. It is the ongoing
compression pressure — the global tendency of the dimensional field to remove degrees of
freedom. As long as the compression process continues (which is always — compression is
irreversible and monotonic), there is a persistent pressure that drives lateral
redistribution.

This resolves the cosmological constant problem conceptually: Λ is not a vacuum energy
waiting to be calculated from particle physics. It is the global compression rate — a
property of the process, not a property of the vacuum. Its value is set by the current
state of the global compression budget, which evolves on cosmological timescales.

**The acceleration is structural.**

Why does the expansion accelerate? Because the competition changes over time.

Early universe: matter density is high → compression gradients between stable structures
(gravity) dominate → expansion decelerates.

Late universe: matter thins out → compression gradients weaken → the background
compression pressure (dark energy) dominates → expansion accelerates.

This is not a coincidence requiring anthropic reasoning. It is the natural consequence of
two competing effects that scale differently with the expansion: matter density falls as
a⁻³ while the compression pressure (Λ) is approximately constant. The crossover epoch
when compression pressure starts winning is determined by the ratio of matter density to
Λ at early times — which in this model is set by the initial compression budget partition.

**The Hubble tension as a compression rate variation.**

If the compression rate (Λ) is not a true constant but a slowly evolving parameter — the
current value of a compression rate that has been decreasing since the first bifurcation
— then early-universe and late-universe measurements of H₀ would naturally differ. The
CMB measures H₀ at recombination (z ≈ 1100) using the early compression rate; the
distance ladder measures it today. A compression rate that decreases slowly over cosmic
time would produce exactly the observed discrepancy: higher H₀ in the past, lower H₀ today.

## Formal Equations

### The DFC Friedmann Equation

The standard Friedmann equation:
```
H² = (8πG/3c²) ρ_total − kc²/a²
```

In DFC interpretation:
```
H² = (8πG/3c²)(ρ_closures + ρ_radiation + ρ_Λ) − kc²/a²
```

where:
- `ρ_closures` = stable kink density (matter — baryons + dark matter)
- `ρ_radiation` = D2-mode propagation density (photons + neutrinos)
- `ρ_Λ`        = compression pressure density (dark energy)
- `k`          = spatial curvature (predicted k = 0 from uniform compression)

The DFC model predicts k = 0 (spatial flatness) because the compression process acts
uniformly at cosmological scales — there is no preferred direction for folding. This is
consistent with observation: Ω_total = 1.000 ± 0.004 (Planck 2018).

evolves with the scale factor:

| Component | w | ρ ∝ a^n |
|---|---|---|
| Radiation | +1/3 | a⁻⁴ |
| Matter | 0 | a⁻³ |
| Cosmological constant (Λ) | −1 | constant |
| Quintessence | −1 < w < −1/3 | a^(−3(1+w)) |

**DFC prediction:** The compression pressure has equation of state:

```

where ε(t) is a small, positive, slowly evolving parameter proportional to the rate of

others).

This is the primary testable prediction: **w ≠ −1** at measurable precision.

Current observational constraint: w = −1.03 ± 0.03 (Planck 2018 + BAO + SNIa).
DFC predicts |ε| < 0.03 today, with ε > 0 (w slightly above −1).

### Scale Factor Evolution

The Friedmann equation can be written in terms of density parameters:

```

For ε > 0, f(z) grows slowly with redshift — the compression pressure was slightly

## Analogy: Re-tiling a Floor

Imagine a floor made of tiles. The tiles represent D3 localization — they define where
things can "be." Objects sitting on the tiles (galaxies) are anchored to specific tiles.

Now imagine the tiles are continuously multiplying — new tiles appear between existing
tiles, pushing them apart. The objects anchored to the tiles do not move relative to
their own tile, but their separation from other objects (measured in tile-widths) increases.

From an object's perspective: all other objects appear to be moving away, with velocity
proportional to distance (more tiles between = faster apparent recession). There is no
center — every object sees the same thing.

- **The tiles multiplying** → lateral redistribution (D3 re-tiling)
- **The rate of multiplication** → Hubble constant
- **The multiplication accelerating** → compression pressure dominating
- **Objects anchored to tiles** → matter structures (D4+ anchored)
- **The floor itself not expanding into anything** → D3 has no external container

**Where the analogy breaks down:** A real floor exists in a pre-existing room. The D3
layer does not exist inside anything — it is the localization layer, and there is no
container around it. The re-tiling happens because dimensional volume is being removed
from below, not because space is pushing outward.
