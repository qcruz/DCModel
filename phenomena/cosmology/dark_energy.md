# Phenomenon: Dark Energy

## One-Sentence Synthesis

> Dark energy is the residual compression pressure of the compression budget that has not
> been captured into stable closure — the ongoing drive of the compression field that
> manifests as the accelerating expansion of the universe.

---

## Observation

~68% of the universe's energy is dark energy — a smoothly distributed energy that causes
accelerating expansion. Discovered 1998 via supernova distance measurements (Nobel 2011).
Equation of state w ≈ −1 (behaves like a cosmological constant). Density remains roughly
constant as the universe expands.

---

## Standard Explanation

Cosmological constant Λ — Einstein's original term, reinterpreted as the energy of empty
space (vacuum energy). The theoretical prediction from quantum field theory is 10¹²⁰ times
too large (the cosmological constant problem). ΛCDM treats Λ as a free parameter fit to
observations.

## Dimensional Folding Explanation

Dark energy is substantially treated in `phenomena/cosmology/cosmic_expansion.md`.

In DFC: dark energy is the global compression pressure — the total compression budget
minus what has been captured into stable closures (matter). The universe expands because
the compression field has not yet equilibrated — it is still redistributing laterally in
response to the ongoing compression budget. The equation of state w_Λ = −1 + ε with
ε ≈ 0.007 (from the Hubble tension analysis) is a DFC prediction.
