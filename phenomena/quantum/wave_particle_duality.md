# Phenomenon: Wave-Particle Duality

## One-Sentence Synthesis

> Wave-particle duality is dissolved in DFC: there is always the compression field (which
> has wave properties — phase, interference, diffraction) and there are localized
> stabilization events (which have particle properties — definite position, momentum).
> There is no single object that is sometimes one and sometimes the other.

---

## Observation

Electrons, photons, neutrons, and molecules all show interference patterns (wave behavior)
and also land at definite points on detectors (particle behavior). The de Broglie relation
λ = h/p connects the wave property (wavelength) to the particle property (momentum) for
any object.

---

## Standard Explanation

Complementarity (Bohr): wave and particle aspects are complementary descriptions of the
same object. The wavefunction encodes both. Which aspect manifests depends on what is
measured. De Broglie relation: λ = h/p.

---

## Dimensional Folding Explanation

There is no paradox in DFC. The apparent duality has a mechanical resolution:

- **Wave behavior** = the compression field in its extended, near-linear regime, satisfying
  the wave equation with phase, superposition, interference
- **Particle behavior** = a localized kink — a stable closure of the same compression field

These are not two descriptions of one object. They are two regimes of the same physical
field. A photon propagating between source and screen is a wave (extended field mode).
A photon detected at the screen is a localized kink-nucleation event. Neither description
is incomplete — they describe different phases of the same field's behavior.

The de Broglie relation λ = h/p follows from the KG dispersion relation p = ℏk → λ = 2π/k
= h/p. Derived, not postulated (see `phenomena/quantum/interference.md`).
