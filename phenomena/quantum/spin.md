## Observation

Quantum particles have an intrinsic angular momentum called spin, measured in units of ℏ/2.
Fermions (electrons, quarks, neutrinos) have half-integer spin (1/2). Bosons (photons,
W/Z, Higgs, gluons) have integer spin (0, 1, 2). Spin-1/2 particles require a 720° rotation
to return to their original state. Spin is quantized along any measurement axis into 2s+1
values.

---

## Standard Explanation

Spin arises from the representation theory of the Lorentz group. Spin-1/2 particles
transform as spinors under rotations. The spin-statistics theorem: half-integer spin →
fermions (obey Pauli exclusion); integer spin → bosons (can occupy same state).
The Standard Model takes spin as a fundamental input.
