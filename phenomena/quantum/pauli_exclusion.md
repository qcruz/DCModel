## Observation

No two identical fermions can occupy the same quantum state simultaneously. This is why
electrons fill distinct atomic orbitals (chemistry), why neutron stars don't collapse
(neutron degeneracy pressure), and why matter is solid and impenetrable. Bosons have
no such restriction and can pile into the same state (lasers, BEC, superfluidity).

---

## Standard Explanation

Spin-statistics theorem: particles with half-integer spin (fermions) have antisymmetric
wavefunctions under particle exchange; particles with integer spin (bosons) have symmetric
wavefunctions. Antisymmetry → if two particles are in the same state, the wavefunction
is zero → probability zero.
