## Observation

Two particles can be prepared in a correlated quantum state such that measuring one
instantly determines something about the other, regardless of the distance between them.
Bell's theorem (1964) and experiments (Aspect 1982, Zeilinger 2022 Nobel) confirm that
this is a real physical effect, not pre-existing hidden variables. Entanglement cannot
be used to send information faster than light.

---

## Standard Explanation

Two particles in a joint quantum state |ψ⟩ = (|↑↓⟩ − |↓↑⟩)/√2 that cannot be factored
into individual particle states. Measurement of one collapses the joint state. Non-locality
is real (Bell inequality violation) but cannot be used for signaling due to the random
nature of measurement outcomes.
