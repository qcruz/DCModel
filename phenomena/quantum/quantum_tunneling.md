## Observation

Quantum particles can pass through potential barriers that classical particles cannot.
Examples: alpha decay (nucleus tunnels through Coulomb barrier), tunnel diodes, scanning
tunneling microscopy, nuclear fusion in stars (protons tunnel through Coulomb repulsion
at energies below the classical barrier height). The tunneling probability decreases
exponentially with barrier width and height.

---

## Standard Explanation

The wavefunction does not vanish inside a classically forbidden region — it decays
exponentially. There is a nonzero amplitude (and hence nonzero probability) to find
the particle on the other side of the barrier.
