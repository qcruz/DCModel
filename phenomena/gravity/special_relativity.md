## Observation

The speed of light is the same in all inertial frames. Clocks moving relative to an
observer run slower (time dilation). Moving objects are length-contracted along their
direction of motion. Mass and energy are equivalent (E = mc²). Nothing with mass can
reach or exceed c. These effects are negligible at everyday speeds but dominate near c.

---

## Standard Explanation

Einstein (1905): the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames, and c is a
universal constant. The Lorentz transformation replaces the Galilean transformation.
Spacetime is a four-dimensional continuum with the metric ds² = c²dt² − dx² − dy² − dz².
