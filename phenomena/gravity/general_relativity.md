## Observation

Massive objects attract one another across empty space. Light bends around massive objects.
Clocks run slower in stronger gravitational fields. Orbits precess in ways Newtonian gravity
cannot account for. Gravitational waves propagate outward from accelerating masses at the
speed of light. Near extreme masses, tidal forces diverge and spacetime descriptions break
down.

All of this is predicted with extraordinary precision by General Relativity.

## Standard Explanation

GR describes gravity not as a force but as the curvature of spacetime caused by the
presence of mass-energy. The Einstein field equations relate the geometry of spacetime
(encoded in the metric tensor) to the distribution of mass-energy (encoded in the
stress-energy tensor). Objects follow geodesics — the straightest possible paths through
curved spacetime. What Newtonian physics describes as a gravitational force is, in GR, the
natural motion of objects through a curved geometry.

GR is a complete and empirically exact description of these effects. What it does not
provide is a causal explanation for *why* mass-energy curves spacetime, or why the specific
form of the field equations is what it is.

## Dimensional Folding Explanation

The apparent geometry of spacetime is an emergent description of how the D3 localization
layer re-tiles as compression proceeds nonuniformly. The Einstein field equations remain
fully valid as a description of that geometry. What DFC adds is a causal mechanism for
why mass-energy curves the effective geometry and why the specific form of the field
equations is what it is.

A stable compression closure (a particle or mass) resists dimensional collapse. This
resistance creates a localized gradient in the compression rate — the region around the
mass is losing dimensional volume more slowly than the surrounding field. The surrounding
structure must redistribute laterally to accommodate this gradient. That lateral
redistribution pattern, observed from within the D3 localization layer, is what GR
encodes as curvature of the metric.

## Specific Gravitational Phenomena

### Singularities

In classical GR, singularities are points where curvature, density, and tidal forces
diverge. This is understood to signal a breakdown of GR itself — a regime where additional
physics is required.

In the dimensional folding model, singularities are regions where dimensional folding
overwhelms all available higher-dimensional capacity. Rather than representing physical
infinities, a singularity marks:

- The exhaustion of stabilizing dimensional layers
- The forced reversion of structure toward lower-dimensional unity states
- A breakdown of spacetime as a sufficient descriptive framework

This aligns with the existing expectation that GR is incomplete near singularities — but
provides a specific mechanism: the descriptive framework breaks down because the layers
it is built on have collapsed. The singularity is not a place of infinite density. It is
the dimensional saturation limit, where the dimensional stack has folded past the point
where D3 spacetime can describe what remains.

### Event Horizons

An event horizon forms where the inward rate of dimensional folding exceeds the outward
capacity of lower-dimensional propagation modes.

From outside: a causal boundary in spacetime — signals cannot escape.
From the dimensional field: a surface where dimensional loss dominates over lateral
redistribution. The folding rate is fast enough that even D2 modes (light) cannot
propagate outward — the redistribution pathways have been consumed faster than they
can open.

### Hawking Radiation

Near the event horizon, the extreme folding gradient creates a boundary region where
lower-dimensional modes can become partially stabilized just outside the collapsing
structure. The folding gradient allows energy to be shed outward as thermal radiation —
the local smoothing of extreme dimensional gradients at the horizon boundary.

This radiation is thermal because it is governed by geometry and boundary conditions,
not by specific particle interactions. The temperature is set by the gradient steepness
at the horizon — consistent with standard Hawking calculations. The radiation is not
particles created from nothing; it is the horizon-level shedding of collapse-induced
structure into lower-dimensional propagation channels.

### Gravitational Redshift

Light is a near-pure D2 mode propagating along lower-dimensional boundaries. When it
travels from a region of stronger folding (near a massive object) to a region of weaker
folding (far from it), it crosses regions with different rates of dimensional volume
removal.

In stronger-folding regions:
- The effective dimensional capacity available to the D2 mode decreases
- Energy is redistributed across modes as the field adjusts to the local folding rate
- Observers in weaker-folding regions measure less energy per cycle — redshift

This preserves the GR equivalence between gravitational redshift and time dilation, while
grounding redshift in dimensional energy redistribution: the D2 mode loses available
degrees of freedom as it climbs out of a high-folding region, and the energy loss appears
as a frequency decrease.

## Analogy: River Current and Stones

Imagine a river whose current varies in speed across its width. Objects floating in the
river do not follow straight lines — they curve toward the faster-moving regions because
those regions remove the water they are sitting in more quickly, allowing surrounding
water to push them laterally.

Place stones on the riverbed — stabilized objects that resist being swept away. The stones
create local disruptions in the current. Surrounding water must re-route around them,
creating eddies and pressure gradients that pull nearby floating objects in.

- The river current → dimensional folding rate (vertical collapse)
- Variation in current speed → folding gradient (uneven collapse)
- Floating objects curving → geodesic motion through re-tiled dimensional space
- Stones → massive objects (stabilized, resisting reconfiguration)
- Eddies and pressure gradients → gravitational field (lateral redistribution pattern)

**Where it breaks down:** Rivers flow through pre-existing space. In this model, there is
no pre-existing space — the dimensional redistribution *is* the space. The river analogy
captures the gradient dynamics but not the emergent nature of the spatial background itself.
