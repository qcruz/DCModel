## Observation

Black holes are regions where gravity is strong enough that even light cannot escape. The
event horizon is the point of no return. Hawking (1974) predicted black holes emit thermal
radiation at temperature T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B). Supermassive black holes (10⁶–10¹⁰ M_☉)
exist at the centers of most galaxies. The merger of two black holes produces gravitational
waves (LIGO 2015).

---

## Standard Explanation

GR solution (Schwarzschild 1916). The event horizon is at r_s = 2GM/c². Beyond it, all
future-pointing geodesics point toward the singularity. Hawking radiation arises from
quantum field theory in curved spacetime — virtual particle pairs near the horizon where
one falls in and the other escapes.
