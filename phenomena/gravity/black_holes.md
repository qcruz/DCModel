## Observation

Black holes are regions where gravity is strong enough that even light cannot escape.
Key observational facts:

- **Event horizon**: at the Schwarzschild radius r_s = 2GM/c², all future-pointing
  paths lead inward; light emitted at r_s never reaches an outside observer
- **Hawking radiation**: black holes emit thermal radiation at temperature
  T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B) (theoretical prediction, Hawking 1974; not directly detected)
- **Bekenstein-Hawking entropy**: S_BH = A/(4L_Pl²) = kc³A/(4Gℏ) — entropy is
  proportional to horizon area, not volume (unique to black holes among known systems)
- **Mass spectrum**: stellar-mass (3–100 M_☉), intermediate (10³–10⁵ M_☉), and
  supermassive (10⁶–10¹⁰ M_☉) black holes observed at galactic centers
- **GW signatures**: binary black hole mergers detected by LIGO from ~400 Mpc (GW150914)
- **Imaging**: M87* and Sgr A* imaged by the Event Horizon Telescope (2019, 2022);
  shadow radius consistent with GR Schwarzschild geometry

## Standard Explanation

**Schwarzschild metric (GR):** The vacuum solution to the Einstein field equations
for a spherically symmetric mass M:

```
ds² = −(1 − r_s/r)c²dt² + dr²/(1 − r_s/r) + r²dΩ²     [r_s = 2GM/c²]
```

At r = r_s, the time component vanishes and the radial component diverges (coordinate
singularity; physical spacetime is smooth there). At r = 0, the curvature invariants
diverge (genuine physical singularity).

**Hawking radiation:** Quantum field theory in curved spacetime. The horizon creates
a temperature gradient in the vacuum state: modes that would be vacuum fluctuations
in flat spacetime appear as real particles to an asymptotic observer, with a thermal
spectrum at T_H. The entropy S_BH = A/4 (in Planck units) follows from demanding
thermodynamic consistency.

The information paradox: as the black hole evaporates via Hawking radiation, does the
quantum information of infalling matter survive in the outgoing thermal radiation, or
is it destroyed? This is unresolved in standard GR + QFT.

### Hawking Radiation: Horizon-Level Compression Shedding

Near the event horizon, the compression gradient is extremely steep — the field changes
rapidly over distances comparable to the horizon curvature scale. This creates a regime

In DFC terms: the horizon is a dimensional boundary under extreme compression stress.

The thermal spectrum of Hawking radiation — with temperature T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B) —

```

Surface gravity κ = c²/(2r_s) = c⁴/(4GM)  [compression gradient rate at horizon]
```

This is consistent but not derived from the DFC field equation. A full derivation

### Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy: Horizon as Dimensional Boundary

The black hole entropy S_BH = A/(4L_Pl²) has a natural DFC interpretation:

The event horizon is a dimensional boundary — the surface at which D3 spatial structure
fails. The number of distinct configurations that can be stored in the horizon surface
is proportional to the area measured in units of L_Pl² (the minimal unit of D3 surface,
set by the kink width). Each Planck-area patch of the horizon can hold one bit of
information about the sub-horizon structure.

This gives S_BH ~ A/L_Pl², which matches the Bekenstein formula up to the factor of 4
(the factor of 4 arises from the precise thermal factor in the Hawking temperature; its
derivation from DFC boundary conditions is open).

The key insight: entropy is proportional to area, not volume, because the degrees of
freedom accessible at the boundary of the collapsed region (the horizon surface) scale
as area, not as the volume inside. This is structurally natural from a DFC perspective:
the D3 layer that would carry volume-scaling degrees of freedom has collapsed; what
remains at the boundary is a 2D surface structure, and 2D structures scale as area.

### Information Paradox: Dimensional Transfer

The standard information paradox: if a pure quantum state (information) falls into a
black hole and the black hole evaporates as thermal (mixed) Hawking radiation, the
final state is mixed, violating unitarity.

DFC structural conjecture: information is not destroyed at the singularity. It is
transferred to sub-D3 modes (D2 and D1) as the D3 layer collapses. The Hawking
radiation carries correlations with these sub-D3 modes that appear thermal to an
observer in D3 spacetime — but the full quantum state, including the sub-D3 structure,
is pure.

This is a structural argument, not a derivation. It requires:
1. A well-defined notion of "information" in sub-D3 modes
2. Showing that the Hawking radiation carries entanglement with sub-D3 structure
3. Demonstrating that the full compression field evolution is unitary

All three steps are open.

## Analogy: Drain Vortex

Imagine a fluid drained by a vortex. Water near the center spirals inward faster and
faster. At some radius, the inward flow speed equals the speed at which surface waves
can propagate outward on the fluid — waves generated inside that radius cannot escape.
An observer on the fluid surface sees this as a "sonic horizon" — the surface-wave
analogue of the event horizon.

- Inward flow speed → compression rate (local dimensional volume removal)
- Surface wave speed → D2 propagation speed c
- Sonic horizon radius → Schwarzschild radius r_s
- Whirlpool interior → sub-horizon spacetime
- Drain → the D1 asymptote (dimensional sink)

**Where it breaks down:** Fluid flows through pre-existing space; the drain is a
spatial location. In DFC, there is no background space — the horizon forms where
dimensional volume removal exceeds redistribution capacity in a self-referential
geometry. The analogy captures the critical-speed-threshold character of the horizon
but misses the emergent-space aspect.

(This drain-vortex model is also the basis for "dumb hole" acoustic black hole analogs,
which have been experimentally realized and show Hawking-like phonon emission — indirect
experimental support for the thermal-boundary-at-critical-flow-speed mechanism.)

## Open Questions

1. **Derive Hawking temperature from DFC field dynamics.** The standard derivation uses
   Bogoliubov transformation between in- and out-vacuum states in curved spacetime.
   The DFC version: compute how the compression field vacuum state before horizon
   formation maps to the vacuum state of an asymptotic observer after horizon
   formation. This requires the full DFC field equation in Schwarzschild background.

2. **Derive the factor of 4 in S_BH = A/(4L_Pl²).** The area-scaling of entropy is
   structurally natural in DFC (D3 collapse → area-scaling degrees of freedom).
   The factor of 4 comes from the thermal character of the Hawking spectrum (related
   to the surface gravity κ and T_H via κ = 2πk_B T_H/ℏ). Deriving this from DFC
   boundary conditions on the collapsed D3 layer is open.

3. **Information paradox resolution from sub-D3 modes.** The structural conjecture
   (information transferred to D2/D1 modes at the singularity) needs to be formalized:
   (a) Define what "information" means in sub-D3 structure; (b) Show the DFC compression
   field evolution is unitary even through D3 collapse; (c) Verify Hawking radiation
   carries entanglement with sub-D3 structure.

4. **Primordial black holes as dark matter candidates.** Black holes with M ~ 10^17–10^23 kg
   formed during radiation domination could constitute part or all of the dark matter.
   DFC's dark matter candidate is D4–D5 fractional-depth kinks (~35 keV); primordial
   BHs are a separate candidate. Whether both can coexist, or whether one excludes the
   other, depends on the DFC account of structure formation.
