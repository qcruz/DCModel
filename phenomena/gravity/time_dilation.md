## Observation

Clocks run slower in stronger gravitational fields (gravitational time dilation — confirmed
by GPS systems, Pound-Rebka experiment) and when moving at high speed (velocity time
dilation — confirmed by muon lifetime measurements, atomic clocks on aircraft). The effect
is given by t' = t × √(1 − 2GM/rc² − v²/c²). GPS satellites must correct for both effects.

---

## Standard Explanation

GR: time is the timelike component of the spacetime metric. In a gravitational field,
g₀₀ = 1 − 2GM/rc², slowing proper time. SR: moving clocks tick slower by the Lorentz
factor γ = 1/√(1 − v²/c²).

## Dimensional Folding Explanation

*Partially addressed in `phenomena/gravity/general_relativity.md`.*

**Expected DFC account:** Time is not a dimension but the bookkeeping of irreversible
compression events. Time dilation is the slowing of that bookkeeping rate: in a stronger
compression gradient (gravitational field), the rate at which the compression field
produces irreversible events is modified. In a moving frame, the field's natural
propagation speed c means irreversible events accumulate more slowly in the direction
of motion.
