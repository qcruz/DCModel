## Observation

Every object at temperature T emits thermal radiation whose spectrum depends only on T,
not on the object's composition. The peak wavelength shifts with temperature (Wien's law:
λ_max = b/T, b = 2.898 × 10⁻³ m·K). The total power scales as T⁴ (Stefan-Boltzmann).
The Planck spectrum was the first quantum result (Planck 1900): u(ν) = 8πhν³/c³ × 1/(e^{hν/kT} − 1).

---

## Standard Explanation

Thermal equilibrium distribution of photons (massless bosons) in a cavity. The Bose-Einstein
distribution n(ν) = 1/(e^{hν/kT} − 1) gives the mean photon number at frequency ν.
Planck's quantum hypothesis E = hν was required to avoid the "ultraviolet catastrophe" of
classical theory.
