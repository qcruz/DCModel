## Observation

Matter transitions sharply between phases (solid, liquid, gas, plasma) at specific
temperatures and pressures. First-order transitions (melting, boiling) involve latent heat.
Second-order (continuous) transitions (ferromagnetism, superconductivity, Bose-Einstein
condensation) involve diverging susceptibility and scale-free fluctuations (universality
classes). The Higgs transition is a phase transition in the electroweak sector.

---

## Standard Explanation

Statistical mechanics: a phase transition occurs when the free energy has a non-analytic
point — the thermodynamic limit of a many-body system. Landau theory describes continuous
transitions via an order parameter and effective potential. Universality: different systems
share the same critical exponents if they share the same symmetry and dimensionality.
