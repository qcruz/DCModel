\section{Glossary of DFC-specific terms}
\label{app:glossary}

\textbf{Compression field $\phi$:} Scalar (or minimal set) encoding local compression state.

[Dimensionality: In the toy model, [$\phi$] = dimensionless; in a complete theory, potentially [length$^{-1}$] or [mass]]

\textbf{Compression budget $B$:} Extensive bookkeeping quantity integrating a model-dependent density $\rho_B$.

[Dimensionality: [energy] or [action/time], depending on formalization]

\textbf{Buckling instability:} Transition opening orthogonal degrees of freedom when compression exceeds stability thresholds.

[Threshold scale: $C_{\text{crit}}$, dimensionally similar to $\phi^2$]

\textbf{Closure:} Stable localized folded configuration satisfying loop-consistency $\oint \delta\phi \approx 0$ (schematic).

[Characteristic size: $\lambda_{\text{closure}} \sim \lambda_{\text{kink}}$ in toy model; potentially $\sim\ell_P$ in Planck regime]

\textbf{Dimensional depth $D$:} Intensive measure of compression intensity/reduced configurational freedom (not an extra dimension).

[Dimensionality: Could be defined as $D \equiv \log(\rho_B/\rho_{B,\text{vacuum}})$ making it dimensionless]

\textbf{Re-tiling:} Coherent adjustment of local folding alignment induced by closure density (intended to map to curvature/connection).

[Rate scale: $\partial_t(\text{alignment}) \sim \rho_{\text{closure}}$, analogous to how $\partial_t g_{\mu\nu} \sim T_{\mu\nu}$ in GR]

\section{Speculative connection to information theory}
\label{app:information}

Several reviewers of preliminary versions noted parallels between DFC's compression budget and information-theoretic entropy. While DFC does not currently formalize this connection, we note the following suggestive correspondences:

\textbf{1. Budget as information capacity:} If $B$ measures ``available configurational storage,'' it may relate to the Shannon entropy bound $S_{\text{info}} \leq A/(4\ell_P^2)$ for bounded regions (Bekenstein bound).

\textbf{2. Closure stability as error correction:} Stable closures must maintain self-consistency under perturbations—analogous to quantum error-correcting codes that preserve information against noise.

\textbf{3. Entanglement as shared folding identity:} If entangled states share deeper substrate layers, mutual information $I(A:B)$ might map to the overlap integral of their respective folding configurations.

These connections remain purely conceptual. A formalized DFC would need to show whether:
\begin{itemize}
\item The compression budget $B$ is related to von Neumann entropy by a fixed conversion factor,
\item Holographic entropy bounds emerge naturally from substrate mechanics,
\item Quantum error correction structures appear in the stability conditions for closures.
\end{itemize}

We list this as a medium-priority research direction, contingent on first resolving the critical gaps around quantum mechanics (\S\ref{sec:limits}).
