\section{Cosmological considerations (expanded gaps and targets)}
\label{sec:cosmology}

\subsection{Expansion as stress relief (now framed as target constraints)}

DFC interprets expansion as large-scale buckling/stress relief, but currently lacks:
\begin{itemize}
\item an equation of state,
\item a derivation of acceleration,
\item a mapping to $H(z)$ evolution.
\end{itemize}

A future model would need to define how global budget density couples to an effective scale factor, and under what conditions ``relief'' accelerates rather than decelerates.

\textit{Specific requirement for future models:} If we model the universe as having an effective compression density $\rho_B(t)$ and pressure $p_B(t)$ arising from substrate stress, then the expansion history is governed by:
\begin{equation}
H^2 \propto \rho_B, \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{d\rho_B}{dt} = -3H(\rho_B + p_B).
\end{equation}

For accelerating expansion ($d^2a/dt^2 > 0$), we require $p_B < -\rho_B/3$, which corresponds to an equation of state $w = p_B/\rho_B < -1/3$.

In DFC terms, this would mean that stress relief (which manifests as negative pressure) dominates over compression storage (positive energy density). A concrete mechanism might involve: as buckling creates new degrees of freedom faster than compression can fill them, the resulting ``stretching'' produces effective negative pressure. However, deriving $w \approx -1$ (cosmological constant) or explaining why this dominates only at late times remains an open challenge.

\subsection{Horizons (retained)}

Horizons are treated as boundaries where alignment propagation is outpaced by local re-tiling demand, producing effective causal disconnection.

\subsection{Early universe (new, explicitly open)}

DFC replaces singular ``initial conditions'' with a saturation/buckling regime, but this draft does not model:
\begin{itemize}
\item inflation-like behavior,
\item primordial perturbations,
\item CMB observables.
\end{itemize}

These are identified as critical future tests of coherence.

\section{Testability and distinguishing scenario-level implications (replacing vague falsifiability)}
\label{sec:testability}

DFC is not predictive in this draft, but it can articulate distinguishing directions that a formalized model would have to commit to. Examples:

\textbf{1. Black hole information release mechanism:} DFC suggests information leaks via substrate boundary modes rather than being destroyed or perfectly preserved in Hawking radiation. A formalized model must specify:
\begin{itemize}
\item The timescale $\tau_{\text{leak}}$ for information transfer to boundary modes as a function of black hole mass $M$: does $\tau_{\text{leak}} \propto M$, $M^2$, or $M^3$?
\item Whether the entanglement entropy between interior and exterior modes follows the Page curve exactly or shows deviations at $O(1/M)$ level.
\item Whether late-time Hawking radiation shows correlations inconsistent with pure thermal emission at sensitivity levels potentially accessible to future gravitational wave/electromagnetic observations of black hole mergers or primordial black hole evaporation.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{2. Planck-regime dispersion:} If compression dynamics introduce a characteristic $\ell_c$, a completed model might entail modified dispersion relations near $E_{\text{crit}} \sim \hbar c/\ell_c$. The draft does not assert such a modification but flags it as a natural discriminator.

\textbf{3. Entanglement/geometry scaling:} If ``dimensional depth'' corresponds to an intensive compression variable, a completed DFC model could predict specific scaling of entanglement entropy with boundary size across regimes (e.g., area-law to modified exponents) analogous in spirit to entanglement–geometry programs \cite{swingle2012}.

These are intentionally framed as scenario-level discriminators contingent on future formalization.

\section{Limits and open problems (strengthened ``must acknowledge'' list)}
\label{sec:limits}

Current omissions (non-exhaustive):
\begin{enumerate}
\item No derivation of Einstein field equations.
\item No emergence account for the Schrödinger equation or complex amplitudes.
\item No origin of $\hbar$, gauge group structure, coupling constants, or anomalies.
\item No particle spectrum, CP violation, or matter–antimatter asymmetry mechanism.
\item No cosmological quantitative mapping (dark energy equation of state; $H(z)$).
\item No explicit microphysical substrate specification.
\end{enumerate}

\textit{Priority ranking for future work:}

\textbf{Critical (must address for minimal viability):}
\begin{itemize}
\item Items 2 and 3: Complex amplitudes, $\hbar$, and Schrödinger equation emergence
\end{itemize}

\textbf{High priority (required for serious consideration):}
\begin{itemize}
\item Items 1 and 5: Einstein equations and cosmological evolution
\item Item 6: Substrate specification (at least at toy model level)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Medium priority (important but can await critical progress):}
\begin{itemize}
\item Item 4: Particle spectrum and CP violation
\end{itemize}

The framework's value depends primarily on resolving the critical-priority items, as these determine whether DFC is compatible with quantum mechanics at a fundamental level.
